Study Abroad

During the spring of 2015, when completing my first undergraduate degree at Gonzaga University, I had the opportunity to spend 4 months studying abroad in _______________. Classes were held at Gonzaga's own campus Monday through Thursday, giving us 3 day weekends to explore Europe. There were school-sponsored trips and events hosted locally and in other countries, but otherwise we were given the choice to choose where we would like to spend any given weekend. Below are a few unique experiences I had in different places across Europe.

  • _______________

We visited this location over Easter weekend. We'd heard all about the famous Irish pubs and were excited to tour the Guinness factory. To our surprise, we learned that in Ireland it was illegal to sell alcohol on Good Friday, which of course happened to be the first full day of our weekend trip. We were able to rearrange our plans to travel outside of the city and sight-see some smaller towns, and save our drinking adventures for Saturday. We later learned the prohibition of the sale of alcohol on Good Friday was lifted in 2018.

  • _______________

We visited this city as part of a school sponsored trip during our first weekend abroad. Part of this included a tour at Vatican City, and on that Sunday we got to stand in St. Peter's Square to see Pope Francis greet and pray over the crowd. While waiting for the event to start, I got to talking to someone I hadn't met before and we've remained friends since, and we get to tell people we met while waiting to see the Pope.

  • _______________

For this weekend trip, we signed up through a student tourism organization called "Florence for Fun." It was called the Winter Wonderland tour and stopped in a few cities over the weekend that included a lot of beautiful mountainous scenery. In this particular city, we got to go on the Sound of Music tour where we saw some of the original filming locations, and had dinner at Augistiner Brau, where monks have been brewing beer since 1621.
